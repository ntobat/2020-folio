KINIS

Visual Identity

The visual identity of a brand is important for awareness, consistency, and the development of an emotional bond between the company and its customers.

01   _OVERVIEW


_CONTEXT /

Kinis is a barefoot apparel startup based in Richmond, VA. They launched with a prototype and a mission: raise awareness about the benefits of barefoot exercise and equip people with the footwear necessary to make the switch. I was brought on to develop their brand identity and style guide.


_CHALLENGE /

The challenge here was to create mountains out of a molehill. The founder was extremely passionate about his product and mission, and he had some initial assets to work off of, but he was not great at articulating his vision for the brand identity. There was a lot of trial and error, but hey, that's what design is all about.


_CONCEPT /

Kinis is all about the future of exercise and the movement to barefoot training. Much of the research supporting barefoot exercise hearkens to the habits and practices of our ancestors, who lived their entire lives sans shoes. Therefore, the brand identity pays respect to early human cave art while utilizing sleek and modern curves to link past and future.

02   _DESIGN


_COMPONENTS /

The actual task given to me was quite vague; a "brand identity" can include or exclude many things. In this case, I inluded the following:

  • 1.   Logo
  • 2.   Wordmark
  • 3.   Color Palette
  • 4.   Typography
  • 5.   Icon & Imagery guidelines
