Vertebrae: Design System

Overview

Vertebrae is the Design System for Backbone PLM. I embarked on its creation in my first few months on the job because of the inconsistencies and incomplete implementations I noticed plaguing our app. I believed a design system was the perfect way to address these issues while simultaneously strengthening our brand and the designer/developer relationship.

Crafting a Design System

1. Motivation

One of my first projects was to design a new feature for the Bill of Materials (or BOM; essentially, a spreadsheet of all the components it takes to make a product, such as a t-shirt). You can see it in the first image below. And if you do open it up, you should immediately notice some glaring UI issues: the toolbar is not attached to the nav bar; column headers are not aligned with their columns; icon and input alignments are off. As I explored the BOM, my immediate urge was to redesign it; it clearly could use some TLC. But as I grew more familiar with the app, it became more and more obvious that there was a larger problem afoot — there was no consistency across our design patterns, and the designs being passed to engineering were not being properly implemented.

2. The Audit

I have created plenty of style guides, but never something so involved as a design system, combining design, code, and branding in one place. After researching best practices for design systems, I knew my first step would be to audit ... everything. Our UI Kit, the style guide, our marketing website, the ENTIRE app, resuable code components, stylesheets — the whole enchilada.

3. Creation

I named the system Vertebrae; the vertebrae are the bones that form the backbone, making a fitting analogy.
